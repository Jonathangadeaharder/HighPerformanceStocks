# Discarded Thematic ETF Candidates (March 2026)

The following stocks were identified via thematic ETF holdings (Robotics, Cybersecurity, Emerging Markets, etc.) but failed to meet the strict ~20% forward EPS growth threshold required for the "Quality Torque" strategy. They are recorded here for future reference and re-evaluation.

## Robotics & Industrial Automation

- **Cognex (CGNX)**: Discarded. Despite near-term margin expansion, the machine vision market is only growing at ~9-10% annually. Long-term normalized EPS growth sits around 10-15%, failing the ~20% torque requirement.
- **Teradyne (TER)**: Discarded. While seeing a near-term 75% pop driven by AI test equipment, this is a highly cyclical semiconductor testing business. The growth is a cyclical upswing rather than sustainable ~20% secular compounding.

## Cybersecurity

- **Fortinet (FTNT)**: Discarded. Billings growth has decelerated to ~13%. While a high-quality incumbent, it lacks the hyper-growth velocity required for the strategy.
- **Okta (OKTA)**: Discarded. Forward guidance shows revenue and EPS growth decelerating to single digits (~9%). The identity management market is maturing and facing intense Microsoft competition.

## Emerging Markets

- **Sea Limited (SE)**: Discarded. While revenue growth remains strong, the company faces brutal direct competition from Lazada and TikTok Shop in Southeast Asia, leading to unpredictable margin profiles and a recent earnings selloff. Lacks the clearer monopolistic dominance of MercadoLibre (MELI).

## Serial Acquirers / Industrials

- **IDEX Corporation (IEX)**: Discarded. A solid serial acquirer, but long-term organic revenue growth is only ~2.8% to 8%, translating to EPS growth well below the 20% hurdle. More suitable for a low-beta dividend growth portfolio than a high-torque strategy.
